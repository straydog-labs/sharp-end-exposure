How it works
Sharp End Exposure is a somatic check-in before risk. The goal: internalize this check until you run it mentally at the base of every route without opening your phone.
Three activation zones
Depth from center
= activation level
Comfort
Route demands appear within current experience. Lower activation.
Learning
Route at the edge of current experience. Optimal for skill acquisition. The fringe of this zone is where the most meaningful growth occurs.
Panic
Perceived demand exceeds current experience. Elevated activation may narrow attentional focus and affect fine motor control.
Based on the Yerkes-Dodson inverted-U model of arousal and performance. Moderate activation produces peak output; too little or too much narrows it. Yerkes, R.M. & Dodson, J.D. (1908). Journal of Comparative Neurology and Psychology.
01 — Body first
The first thing this tool asks you to do is breathe and notice your gut — before any questions about the route. Body before brain. The somatic check-in is the whole point. The questions build context around what your body already knows.
02 — Two signals, asked separately
Breathing and gut are asked separately — two distinct physiological systems. Combining them into one question produces worse data. Each is a separate moment of noticing.
03 — The delta is the data
The tool asks about your somatic state twice — before and after you think through the route. The shift between those two moments is the primary output. A shift tells you something specific about this route, right now. No shift also tells you something.
04 — Your data, mirrored back
The result leads with what your specific answers showed — not a generic zone description. If your breathing tightened from effortless to restricted after thinking about this route, that is what you see. Specific data produces specific awareness.
Answer based on current state, not aspirational state. Zone placement carries no value judgment. Comfort, learning, and panic reflect activation levels — not character or capability. The decision about what to attempt remains yours entirely.
