How it works
Four design principles. Each one maps directly to how the body actually processes risk — not how we assume it should.
Three activation zones
Comfort
Route demands within current experience. Lower activation.
Learning
Route at the edge of experience. The fringe is where growth peaks.
Panic
Perceived demand exceeds experience. Activation may affect judgment.
Based on the Yerkes-Dodson inverted-U model of arousal and performance. Yerkes, R.M. & Dodson, J.D. (1908). Journal of Comparative Neurology and Psychology.
Design principle
Body before brain
The first thing this tool asks is for you to breathe and notice your gut — before any questions about the route.
▸
The somatic check-in is the whole point. Everything else — questions about style, experience, route — builds context around what your body already knows. If you skip the body and go straight to context, you produce a score. If you start with the body, you produce awareness. These are not the same thing.
Two distinct systems
Breath and gut — asked separately
Two different physiological channels. Combining them into one question produces worse data than asking each alone.
▸
Breathing and gut sensation are anatomically and functionally distinct. The breath follows respiratory rhythm and vagal tone. The gut follows the enteric nervous system — sometimes called the second brain. Asking about them in a single combined question causes people to anchor on whichever signal feels stronger and discount the other. Two questions, two moments of noticing, two separate data points.
Primary output
The shift is the signal
The tool asks about your somatic state twice. The difference between those two moments leads your result — not your zone placement.
▸
No shift is also data. If your breathing stays effortless and your gut stays settled after thinking through the route, that tells you something specific. A significant shift — both signals tightening — tells you something different. The tool leads with the delta, not the zone, because the shift is what is actionable. Your zone tells you where you are. Your shift tells you what just happened to your body when it considered this specific route.
Specific, not generic
Your data, mirrored back
The result describes what your specific answers showed — not a generic zone description that could apply to anyone.
▸
Generic zone text is wallpaper. It does not help you decide anything because it does not reflect anything specific about you, this route, or this moment. The result screen leads with your exact shift described in your exact language. If your breathing went from effortless to restricted and your gut went from settled to knotted, that is what it says — because that is what happened, and that is what matters.
Full body scan
Head to toe — before the route enters the picture
After breath and gut, a full scan for what you're already holding. Route thinking changes what you notice — the scan captures baseline before that happens.
▸
The body scan happens at a specific moment: after the first two somatic questions, before any questions about the route. That order is intentional. Thinking through a route changes what you notice in your body — the scan captures baseline physical state before that happens.

Five regions, tap any that stand out. Two follow-up questions: what you are noticing most, and whether this is typical for you before climbing. The second question is what makes the data useful over time — chronic shoulder tension that is your normal baseline is different data from elevated tension that shows up today specifically.

Nothing to report is equally valid. A clean baseline is useful data too.
Body scanning as a structured pre-activity practice is grounded in MBSR research. Directed attention to body regions surfaces sub-threshold holding patterns that correlate with activation state and injury risk. Kabat-Zinn (1990) · Levine (1997) · Price & Hooven (2018)
Answer based on current state, not aspirational state. Zone placement carries no value judgment — it is a readout of activation relative to this route, right now. The decision about what to attempt remains entirely yours.

The Research
Ten studies across four themes. Every design decision in this tool maps directly to peer-reviewed science.
Somatic signals
The body is already reporting
Breath and gut register threat before conscious awareness. Biology, not metaphor — and trainable.
▸
2017Lisa Feldman Barrett — How Emotions Are Made
Emotions are not hardwired reactions — they are constructed from interoceptive signals, past experience, and cultural context. The same physiological activation can be experienced as excitement or terror depending on how the brain interprets it.
Why the tool never labels somatic signals as "fear." Restricted breathing is described as restricted breathing — not as danger. How you interpret that signal is yours to determine.
1994Antonio Damasio — Descartes' Error
Gut sensation functions as a pre-conscious decision marker. Patients with intact reasoning but damaged somatic signal pathways made systematically worse decisions — even when they could explain the correct choice. The gut reports before the mind catches up.
The pause screen creates conditions where gut markers can register before the second check-in. The delta card reports gut shift explicitly — because it is the primary pre-decision data point.
2015Sarah Garfinkel & Hugo Critchley — Biological Psychology
Interoceptive accuracy — correctly detecting internal body signals — is distinct from simply being aware you are noticing something. Accuracy varies across individuals, including along neurodivergent lines, and is measurable and trainable over time.
Why the somatic anchor screen asks you to notice before answering, and why "not paying attention to my breath" is a valid response — not a failure. The tool trains the noticing, not the result.
Arousal & performance
There is an optimal activation window — and it varies by person
Too little produces disengagement. Too much produces breakdown. The window is real — but not universal.
▸
1908Yerkes & Dodson — Journal of Comparative Neurology
Performance follows an inverted-U curve relative to arousal. The optimal window varies by task complexity — fine motor and judgment tasks require lower arousal. Foundational, replicated across more than a century.
The three-zone model is a direct application. Zone placement is a readout of where you sit on the arousal axis relative to this route today — not a capability ranking.
2001Sian Beilock & Thomas Carr — Journal of Experimental Psychology
When pressure activates conscious monitoring of automated movement, expert performance breaks down. Golfers, gymnasts, and athletes across sports and genders show the same pattern — increased self-focus disrupts procedural skill.
Why "distracted" and "reactive" are the panic-zone failure modes. Expert movement is disrupted by exactly the over-monitoring that high activation triggers.
1997Claude Steele — American Psychologist, Stanford
Identity threat — awareness of potential judgment through a negative stereotype — raises activation and impairs performance independently of actual capability. Documented across Black students, women in math, and anyone performing where belonging feels conditional.
Why zone placement carries no value judgment and language throughout the tool is deliberately neutral. A climber managing identity threat is already carrying extra activation this tool must not add to.
Deliberate practice
What you notice during practice determines what you keep
Volume does not predict adaptation. Directed attention does. One deliberate variable outperforms passive repetition every time.
▸
1993Ericsson, Krampe & Tesch-Römer — Psychological Review
Expert performance is not explained by hours alone — it requires deliberate practice: structured repetition with specific process variables identified in advance and immediate feedback. Passive repetition produces plateaus, not expertise.
The process intention question ("What will I pay attention to on the wall?") is the direct application. Comfort zone guidance explicitly notes passive repetition produces limited transfer.
2006Carol Dweck — Mindset: The New Psychology of Success
Research across cultures, genders, and age groups shows that a fixed-capability belief produces worse outcomes than a growth belief. Fixed mindset increases performance anxiety and reduces willingness to attempt challenging tasks.
Why zone placement is never framed as a capability measure. Panic zone means "your body is reporting a gap between perceived demand and current experience." That gap is closable.
2003Arno Ilgner — The Rock Warrior's Way
Conscious risk-taking is a learnable skill. Process focus determines what you take away regardless of outcome. The decision not to attempt is as informative as the decision to proceed. Practitioner framework, not a controlled study.
Did Not Attempt is a first-class option in the climb log. Motivation is an assessment question because conscious vs. reactive motivation produces different learning outcomes.
Body scanning
The body holds what the mind hasn't noticed yet
Tension, fatigue, and holding patterns in specific regions are data — surfaced through directed attention before they become pain.
▸
1990Jon Kabat-Zinn — Full Catastrophe Living
Mindfulness-Based Stress Reduction centres the body scan as a core practice — systematic top-to-foot attention to surface held tension and activation states. Hundreds of RCTs document measurable increases in body awareness and reductions in stress-related holding. The practice improves detection of sub-threshold sensation before it becomes pain or injury.
The body scan screen follows the MBSR sequence: directed attention top-to-bottom, noticing without judgment, reporting what is present. "Nothing to report" is an equally valid and useful outcome — it records a clean baseline.
1997Peter Levine — Waking the Tiger: Healing Trauma
Unresolved activation — stress, threat response, incomplete defensive movement — is held in specific tissue regions as chronic muscular tension below the conscious awareness threshold. Specific body regions act as reservoirs for activation the nervous system has not yet discharged. Practitioner framework with a growing research base.
Why tension in the shoulders before a trad lead reads differently from tension after a gym session. The body scan surfaces what is already present — not what the route caused.
2018Cynthia Price & Carol Hooven — Frontiers in Psychology
Interoceptive awareness skills — noticing, characterising, and responding to body sensation — are teachable and directly improve emotion regulation and decision-making. Participants who developed body awareness skills showed significantly better capacity to identify pre-decision physiological states and act on them appropriately.
Why the body scan asks two follow-up questions: what are you noticing, and is this typical? Characterising and contextualising body sensation is where the skill develops — not just the noticing itself.
A note on methodology
Sharp End Exposure collects self-reported readiness data. It is not a clinical assessment tool. Zone placement reflects the climber's own perception of their state relative to a specific route — not an objective measure of capability. All decisions about what to attempt remain with the climber.
